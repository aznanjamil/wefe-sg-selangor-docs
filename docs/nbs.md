# NBS — Nature-Based Solutions

The **NBS** page shows nature-based solutions for the Sungai Selangor basin — using nature to fix problems that concrete alone cannot solve.

> ![](/img/site/nbs-overview.png)

## Why Nature-Based Solutions?

The basin faces problems that grey infrastructure — dams, walls, pumps — cannot fix alone:

| Problem | What Is Happening |
|---------|-------------------|
| **Floods** | Flood hotspots are multiplying; the river has nowhere to spread |
| **Saltwater intrusion** | Salinity creeps upstream during droughts |
| **Dirty water** | Sediment and fertiliser load the river before it reaches treatment plants |
| **Urban heat** | Townships heat up and shed rainwater faster than drains can carry it |
| **Sediment in dams** | Cleared slopes lose soil that silts up reservoirs |

Nature-based solutions (NbS) restore the natural processes that once regulated the basin for free:
- **Forests** hold water and keep soil in place
- **Floodplains** absorb excess water
- **Wetlands** clean the river
- **Mangroves** protect the coast

> **Key idea:** NbS complement dams and pipes — they do not replace them. They form a second, living layer of infrastructure that grows stronger over time instead of wearing out.

## Four Zones, One System

The basin is split into **four zones**. Each zone has its own pressures and its own natural toolkit.

| Zone | Location | Main Problem | NbS Solutions |
|------|----------|--------------|---------------|
| **Upland slopes** | Hills above Sg. Selangor and Sg. Tinggi dams | Soil erosion, sediment in dams | Agroforestry, Green Corridor |
| **River corridor** | Middle and lower basin | Floods, dirty water, disconnected forests | Floodplain Reconnection, Plant Buffer, Constructed Wetlands |
| **Urban south** | Rawang, Kuang, Serendah, Bukit Beruntung | Heat, flash floods, runoff | Urban Green Index, Rain Gardens, Bioswales |
| **Coast** | Kuala Selangor, Tanjung Karang | Saltwater intrusion, erosion, lost habitat | Mangrove rehabilitation |

## The Solutions

### 1. Agroforestry (Upland Slopes)

**What it is:** Native trees mixed with fruit and timber crops on upland slopes.

**Why it helps:**
- Roots bind the soil so it does not wash into dams
- Canopy slows rainfall before it hits the ground
- Communities (including Orang Asli at Kg. Serendah and Bukit Manchong) earn income without clearing more forest

**Where:** Hills above Sg. Selangor and Sg. Tinggi dams

> ![](/img/site/nbs-agroforestry.png)
---

### 2. Green Corridor (Upland Slopes)

**What it is:** Reconnecting forest reserves with replanted links and wildlife crossings over roads.

**Why it helps:**
- HS Gading, HS Ulu Selangor, HS Bukit Kutu, and HS Batang Kali are being cut into islands by roads and development
- A connected forest regulates rainfall that fills the dams
- Wildlife can move between reserves

**Where:** Between fragmented forest reserves in the upper basin

> ![](/img/site/nbs-green-corridor.png)

---

### 3. Floodplain Reconnection (River Corridor)

**What it is:** Setting bunds back and reopening old channels and disused mining pools.

**Why it helps:**
- The river has been squeezed between bunds for decades
- With nowhere to spread, floods rise faster and hit downstream communities harder
- Reconnected floodplains let water spill, slow, and soak in
- Recharges groundwater and rebuilds fish habitat in dry months
- **Combined with coastal floodplain management** — protects both river and coastal areas from flooding

**Where:** Around Bestari Jaya, Batang Berjuntai, and coastal floodplain areas

> ![](/img/site/nbs-floodplain.png)

---

### 4. Plant Buffer (River Corridor)

**What it is:** A strip of native grasses, shrubs, and trees along the riverbank.

**Why it helps:**
- The lower river runs past paddy fields, picking up fertiliser, pesticide, and sediment
- The buffer filters runoff before it reaches the water
- Roots hold the bank against erosion
- Canopy cools the water
- Doubles as a thin wildlife corridor

**Where:** Along the lower river, past paddy plains

> ![](/img/site/nbs-plant-buffer.png)

---

### 5. Constructed Wetlands (River Corridor)

**What it is:** Engineered reed beds and shallow ponds that clean water naturally.

**Why it helps:**
- SSP1, SSP2, and SSP3 treatment plants draw most of the Klang Valley's water from this river
- Cleaner raw water = cheaper and safer treatment
- Wetlands strip nutrients and sediment
- Buffer storm flows
- Create habitat

**Where:** Upstream of WTP intakes and at IADA drainage outfall

> ![](/img/site/nbs-wetlands.png)

---

### 6. Urban Green Index (Urban South)

**What it is:** A minimum tree canopy and green-cover target for new development.

**Why it helps:**
- Rawang, Kuang, Serendah, and Bukit Beruntung are growing fast
- Replacing forest with rooftops and tarmac heats up townships
- Street canopies and urban forests:
  - Cool the heat island
  - Intercept rainfall
  - Clean the air
  - Stitch green links across townships

**Where:** New developments in fast-growing townships

> ![](/img/site/nbs-urban-green.png)

---

### 7. Rain Gardens (Urban South)

**What it is:** Shallow planted basins in housing estates, schools, and car parks.

**Why it helps:**
- Most urban flooding is local rain with nowhere to go
- Rain gardens catch the first flush of a storm
- Let water soak in instead of rushing to drains
- Filter it on the way down
- Singapore's ABC Waters approach, at neighbourhood scale

**Where:** Housing estates, schools, car parks across urban townships

> ![](/img/site/nbs-rain-gardens.png)

---

### 8. Bioswales (Urban South)

**What it is:** Vegetated channels that replace concrete drains.

**Why it helps:**
- The Gombak edge — Jln KL–Ipoh corridor and Batu Arang–Kuang belt — has flood hotspots
- Hard road verges funnel water fast
- Bioswales:
  - Convey stormwater while slowing it
  - Let water infiltrate into the ground
  - Filter pollutants
- Follow roads already there — no new land needed

**Where:** Road verges in the Gombak edge and urban corridors

> ![](/img/site/nbs-bioswales.png)

---

### 9. Mangrove Rehabilitation (Coast)

**What it is:** Restoring coastal mangrove forests.

**Why it helps:**
- The coast at Banjar Utara, the Nature Park, and Kg. Kuantan has lost mangrove cover
- Mangroves:
  - Blunt storm surge and erosion
  - Slow saltwater intrusion
  - Store blue carbon
  - Nurse fisheries
  - Protect firefly colonies the coast is famous for

**Where:** Banjar Utara, Kuala Selangor Nature Park, Kg. Kuantan belt


## How Solutions Work Together

None of these works alone. They stack from ridge to reef:

| Layer | Solution | Protects What Below It |
|-------|----------|------------------------|
| **Ridge (upland)** | Agroforestry, Green Corridor | Keeps soil out of wetlands and dams |
| **River corridor** | Floodplain Reconnection, Buffer, Wetlands | Makes treatment plants' job easier; takes pressure off the floodplain |
| **Urban** | Green Index, Rain Gardens, Bioswales | Cuts local flooding before it reaches the river |
| **Reef (coast)** | Mangroves | Protects everything downstream |

> "The river already knows how to manage itself. NbS just gives it room to."
